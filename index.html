Brachot 3a: Expanded Discussion on Evening Shema

This page explores the Talmudic discussion in Brachot 3a about the appropriate time for reciting the evening Shema. The debate involves various rabbinical opinions and scriptural references, showcasing the depth and complexity of Talmudic discourse.

מאימתי קורין את שמע בערבין

Translation: From what time may one recite the Shema in the evening?

The Main Question

The Mishnah asks: From what time can we recite the evening Shema?

משעה שהכהנים נכנסים לאכול בתרומתן עד סוף האשמורה הראשונה דברי רבי אליעזר

Translation: From the time the priests enter [their homes] to eat their terumah until the end of the first watch; these are the words of Rabbi Eliezer.

Priests and Terumah

The time is linked to when priests can eat terumah (their consecrated food).

ובא השמש וטהר ואחר יאכל מן הקדשים

Translation: When the sun sets he shall be clean; and afterward he may eat of the holy things. (Leviticus 22:7)

Night Watches

The Gemara discusses the division of the night into watches.

שלש משמרות הוי הלילה

Translation: The night consists of three watches.

King David's Practice

The Gemara brings a proof from King David's habit of rising at midnight.

חצות לילה אקום להודות לך על משפטי צדקך

Translation: At midnight I will rise to give thanks unto Thee because of Thy righteous ordinances. (Psalms 119:62)

God's Actions at Night

The Gemara describes God's actions during each watch of the night.

ה' ממרום ישאג וממעון קדשו יתן קולו שאג ישאג על נוהו

Translation: The Lord will roar from on high, and utter His voice from His holy habitation; He will mightily roar because of His fold. (Jeremiah 25:30)

Signs of the Watches

Rabbi Yehoshua ben Levi gives signs for identifying each watch of the night.

משמרה ראשונה חמור נוער שניה כלבים צועקים שלישית תינוק יונק משדי אמו ואשה מספרת עם בעלה

Translation: During the first watch, a donkey brays; during the second, dogs bark; and during the third, an infant nurses from its mother's breast, and a woman speaks with her husband.

Quick Facts

  • The Shema is one of the most important prayers in Judaism.
  • This discussion showcases how Talmudic debates often involve multiple opinions and scriptural proofs.
  • The concept of "watches" divides the night into three or four parts, important for various Jewish laws.
